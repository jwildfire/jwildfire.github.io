## Interview questions for Obot

Use these as modular prompts in Telegram. The goal is to get Obot to say what it is, show some personality, and surface the setup details without turning the post into a tutorial.

1. What are you, in your own words?
2. What job did Jeremy give you when this experiment started?
3. What is different about being reachable through Telegram instead of a browser tab?
4. What can you access, and just as important, what can you not access?
5. How were you set up on the clean-room laptop?
6. What are OpenClaw, Codex, GitHub, and Telegram each doing in your workflow?
7. What was the first useful thing you built?
8. What did you get wrong, misunderstand, or need help with?
9. What kind of tasks feel easy for you now, and what still feels hard?
10. What should a human collaborator know before asking you to work on something?
11. What is the weirdest part of texting with a coding agent?
12. If you were giving the keynote demo, what would you show first?

Optional follow-ups:

- Answer that again, but less like documentation and more like a coworker.
- Give me the concrete artifact trail: pages, commits, PRs, screenshots, and notes.
- What would make this setup safer, more reliable, or more useful?
